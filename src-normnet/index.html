NormSpace v1.0


This model simulates the emergence and diffusion of social norms. It implements the mechanism of Robert Axelrod’s norms game [1] and extends it in a spatial environment. 


The agents in the model, which are bound to single cells in the grid can possess different types of neighbourhoods as defined in Barry Wellman’s theory of community [2]: Little boxes (Ntype=1 in the model tab), glocalization (Ntype=2), and networked individualism (Ntype=3 or 4). 


The model visualisation consists of four displays:  a) defection and cooperation in the environment, b) vengefulness levels of the agents, c) boldness levels of the agents, d) an illustration of the current neighbourhood topology.


[1] Axelrod, R. “An Evolutionary Approach to Norms. “ The American Political Science Review, vol. 80, pp. 1095-1111, Dec. 1986.


[2] Wellman, B. “Little boxes, glocalitzation, and network individualism,” in Digital Cities II: Computational and Sociological Approaches (M. Tanabe, P. v. d. Besselaar, and T. ishida, eds.), no. 2362 in Lecture Notes in Computer Science, pp. 10-25, Springer Berlin Heidelber, Jan. 2002.
